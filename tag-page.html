TERM CLASSIFICATION: CONCEPTUAL

Univocality

The scholarly consensus that the biblical texts represent a plurality of voices and perspectives, rather than a single, unified theological message.

Ref. ID: LEX-042

Scholarly Commentary

format_quote

"The bible doesn't have a single perspective; it's a library of conflicting voices."

Dan McClellan, PhD

Univocality is often assumed by dogmatic readers, but the data consistently demonstrates multivocality. Different authors in different periods had fundamentally different agendas, concerns, and theological commitments.

Summary of Findings

  • verified Contradicts the notion of a monolithic divine author.
  • verified Reveals internal debate between Priestly and Deuteronomic sources.
  • verified Essential for understanding the evolution of Israelite religion.
