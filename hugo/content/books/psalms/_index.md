Psalms comes up on the show as a testing ground for questions about textual history, manuscript plurality, and the instability of what many readers assume is a fixed biblical book. Episodes return to Psalm 82, Psalm 22, and especially Psalm 151 to show how later interpreters negotiated divine plurality, messianic rereadings, and competing textual traditions across the Masoretic Text, Septuagint, and Dead Sea Scrolls.

The hosts also use Psalms to push past familiar devotional readings and toward composition history. Rather than treating the collection as straightforwardly Davidic, they repeatedly frame it as an edited anthology whose superscriptions, order, and expansions preserve evidence of ongoing literary development and later theological reinterpretation.
