Exodus returns on the show because it sits at the intersection of some of the podcast's favorite pressure points: law, liberation, divine violence, textual development, ritual objects, and the gap between later religious certainty and the messier realities of ancient literature. The hosts come back to it when people want clean answers about the Ten Commandments, Pharaoh's hardened heart, the status of a fetus in biblical law, the Ark of the Covenant, or the historical credibility of the exodus narrative itself.

That makes Exodus a recurring laboratory for Data Over Dogma themes. Some discussions focus on source and redaction, as with the Covenant Code and priestly framing; others turn on translation history, especially where the Septuagint and later interpreters reshape how legal texts are read. And because Exodus is so central to later Jewish and Christian imagination, it also becomes a flashpoint for apologetic overreach, whether in claims about archaeology, divine justice, or what the text is supposed to prove about God's character.
