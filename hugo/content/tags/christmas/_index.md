Christmas sits at the intersection of biblical narrative, later tradition, and modern cultural memory. The show keeps returning to it because the standard Nativity scene is really a harmonized collage: Matthew and Luke tell different birth stories, later texts add still more details, and popular retellings flatten all of it into one seamless tableau of shepherds, Magi, stable, star, donkey, and manger.

That makes Christmas one of the clearest examples of how reception history works. Discussions collected here trace how Bethlehem traditions developed, why the Star of Bethlehem attracts both apologetic and pseudo-scholarly speculation, and how non-canonical texts helped supply many of the details people now assume were always in the Bible.
