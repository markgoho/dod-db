The Council of Nicaea matters in this project because it sits at the intersection of theology, empire, and historical memory. It is one of the most frequently invoked moments in popular Christian storytelling, but it is also one of the easiest to flatten into conspiracy, myth, or oversimplified origin stories.

That makes the tag useful for tracing how doctrine actually develops. Discussions under Nicaea tend to pull together the Arian controversy, the Nicene Creed, later Trinitarian debates, the dating of Easter, and the persistent gap between what a famous council did and what later audiences imagine it must have done.
