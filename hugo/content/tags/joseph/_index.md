Joseph sits at a major hinge in the biblical story, connecting the patriarchal cycles in Genesis to later claims about Israel's life in Egypt. Episodes about Joseph regularly focus less on harmonizing the plot than on exposing how the text preserves competing versions of his sale, shifting group identities like Midianites and Ishmaelites, and different attempts to explain how he reached Egypt in the first place.

Joseph also becomes a testing ground for historical claims. Discussions of archaeology, chronology, and later retellings keep returning to how often readers try to anchor Joseph in Egyptian history or smooth out later traditions about his family and burial, even when the evidence points instead to literary development, conflated memory, and retrospective storytelling.
