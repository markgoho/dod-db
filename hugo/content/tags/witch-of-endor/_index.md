Witch of Endor comes up on the show because the story opens a window into ancient Israelite ideas about the dead, divination, and the limits of later labels like "witchcraft." Discussions tagged here usually stress that Saul's visit only makes sense in a world where consulting the dead was intelligible and, in some settings, expected rather than obviously fictional.

The figure also matters as a case study in translation and framing. Calling her a witch imports assumptions about spellcasting and moral panic that the Hebrew text does not require, while the episode itself centers on necromancy, ancestor veneration, and the politics of deciding which kinds of supernatural access count as legitimate.
