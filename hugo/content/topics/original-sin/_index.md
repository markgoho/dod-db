Original sin anchors a huge amount of later Christian reasoning about death, salvation, human nature, baptism, and why Jesus' atonement is supposed to be necessary for everyone. On the show, it matters less as an isolated doctrine than as a case study in how later theologians assembled scattered biblical passages into a system that answered questions the texts themselves do not answer in one voice.

Debates over original sin also expose how many other doctrines depend on it once it is in place. Discussions of Mary's conception, inherited guilt, the status of infants, and the mechanics of salvation all turn into downstream attempts to solve problems created by earlier doctrinal choices. That makes original sin a recurring flashpoint for the show's larger conversations about multivocal scripture, theological system-building, and the long afterlife of interpretations that became central only well after the biblical texts were written.
