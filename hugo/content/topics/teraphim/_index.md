Teraphim keep resurfacing because they expose how much early Israelite and Judahite religious practice differs from later, cleaner theological retellings. On the show they become evidence for a world in which divine images could sit in homes, appear in legal and ritual settings, and coexist with devotion to YHWH without immediately being framed as scandalous apostasy.

Questions about teraphim also open onto bigger arguments about translation, cult practice, and the development of monotheistic ideology. They help explain why passages about "the gods" or divine images cannot always be flattened into later orthodox categories, and why material religion matters for reading the Bible's older layers on their own terms.
