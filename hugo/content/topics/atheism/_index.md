Atheism recurs on the show wherever debates about religion, identity, and category boundaries turn into struggles over who gets to belong and who gets to define the terms. Discussions collected here push back against the idea that atheist names a timeless, self-evident position, tracing instead how the label shifts from accusations of impiety in the ancient world to modern arguments about belief, evidence, stigma, and public identity.

That makes atheism a useful pressure test for larger questions the show returns to again and again. The topic exposes how badly religious and secular labels break down when people try to force them into neat binaries, and how often identity works through community, practice, and social power rather than through a single creed that everyone inside the category actually shares.
