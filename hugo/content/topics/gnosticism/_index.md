Gnosticism sits near the center of the show's recurring interest in early Christian diversity, heresiology, and the instability of later religious categories. Discussions gathered here use it to show how many competing ways of imagining Jesus, creation, salvation, and the God of Israel were circulating before Nicene Christianity had the power to define orthodoxy and suppress rivals.

Gnosticism also helps expose how messy the category itself is. Episodes tied to this topic return not only to classic features like dualism, secret knowledge, and hostile readings of the material world, but also to the methodological problem that most evidence comes from opponents, later discoveries like Nag Hammadi, and traditions that do not line up neatly enough to yield a single timeless definition.
