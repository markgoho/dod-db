# Quote Bank

Use this file as a reusable source of high-value quote patterns when selecting book-page quotes. Prefer quotes that directly answer questions like:

- How historical is this book or event?
- When was this book likely written or compiled?
- What signs of later editing, layering, or redaction are visible?
- What larger scholarly frame best explains the book?

## Selection rules

- Prefer quotes about authorship, dating, composition history, redaction, textual criticism, ideology, or historicity.
- Prefer quotes that can stand alone without lots of missing context.
- When a strong quote has a vague referent, add a minimal bracketed clarification, e.g. `written after [the period of the judges]`.
- Avoid plot-summary quotes unless they make a historical or compositional point.
- Avoid quotes that are only thematically related to the book but do not illuminate the book itself.
- For each page, try to cover multiple angles across the four quotes: dating, composition, historicity, and interpretive frame.
